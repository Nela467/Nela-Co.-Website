Macro Breakdown & Understanding Balanced Eating

Understanding macronutrients and how to balance your meals is key to supporting your energy, performance, and overall health. Below is a simple guide to help you navigate protein, carbs, and fats in your diet.

Protein

Protein is essential for building and repairing tissues, maintaining muscle mass, and supporting immune function. Sources include lean meats, poultry, fish, eggs, dairy, legumes, and plant-based proteins like tofu and tempeh.

Carbohydrates

Carbs are your body’s main energy source. Focus on complex carbohydrates like whole grains, fruits, vegetables, and legumes. They provide sustained energy and important vitamins, minerals, and fiber.

Fats

Healthy fats are vital for hormone production, brain health, and absorbing fat-soluble vitamins. Include sources such as olive oil, avocado, nuts, seeds, fatty fish, and nut butters in your meals.

Balanced Meals

A balanced meal typically contains a source of protein, complex carbohydrates, healthy fats, and plenty of vegetables. Portion sizes can be adjusted based on your energy needs, activity level, and goals. Aim for variety to ensure you get a wide range of nutrients.

Practical Tips

- Include a protein source in every meal
- Choose whole, minimally processed carbs
- Don’t fear healthy fats, but monitor portions
- Add color and variety with vegetables and fruits
- Listen to your body’s hunger and fullness cues
